Koi Pond Setup Guide

Choosing the Right Location

Select a spot that receives partial shade and is protected from strong winds. Avoid areas under trees to prevent excess debris in the pond.

Pond Size and Depth

Aim for a minimum depth of 4 feet and at least 50 gallons of water per adult koi. A larger pond is generally better for maintaining water quality and fish health.

Filtration System

Install a robust filtration system that includes mechanical, biological, and chemical filtration components. Size the system according to your pond volume and fish load.

Water Quality

Use a dechlorinator when filling the pond and regularly test for pH, ammonia, nitrite, and nitrate levels. Maintain a pH between 7.0 and 8.6 for optimal koi health.

Plants and Decorations

Add aquatic plants to help oxygenate the water and provide natural filtration. Include rocks and other decorations to create hiding spots and enrich the environment.
