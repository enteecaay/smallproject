Koi Health Management Guide

Regular Health Checks

Observe your koi daily for any changes in behavior, appetite, or appearance. Look for signs of stress, such as flashing (rubbing against surfaces) or gasping at the surface.

Common Health Issues

  • Parasites: Look for excessive mucus, scratching, or visible spots on the skin.
  • Bacterial Infections: Watch for red streaks, ulcers, or fin rot.
  • Fungal Infections: Check for cotton-like growths on the body or fins.

Water Quality Management

Maintain excellent water quality to prevent many health issues. Regularly test for ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, and pH levels. Perform partial water changes as needed.

Quarantine Procedures

Always quarantine new fish for at least 2-4 weeks before introducing them to your main pond. This helps prevent the spread of diseases.

When to Seek Professional Help

If you notice persistent health issues or are unsure about treatment, consult a veterinarian specializing in koi or pond fish.
